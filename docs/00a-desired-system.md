# Desired System — My Daniel

**Document:** Proposed Information System Description  
**Source:** Organizational Analysis, Stakeholder Interviews, and Business Process Analysis  
**Prepared by:** Project Team

---

# System Overview

**My Daniel** is a centralized information system designed to support the operational, administrative, and managerial activities of Daniel Rowing Center.

The system aims to replace fragmented manual processes, disconnected information systems, and spreadsheet-based management methods with a single integrated platform that provides real-time information, process automation, and improved decision-making capabilities.

The system will serve multiple stakeholders, including management, coordinators, instructors, accounting staff, maintenance personnel, and customers.

---

# System Goals

The primary goals of the system are:

- Centralize organizational information in a single platform
- Improve operational efficiency
- Reduce manual administrative work
- Improve payroll reporting accuracy
- Streamline customer registration processes
- Improve boat and equipment maintenance management
- Enhance communication between staff and customers
- Provide management with real-time organizational insights

---

# Target Users

| User Role | Main Responsibilities |
|------------|------------|
| Center Manager | Monitor organizational performance, activities, and resources |
| Accounting Manager | Manage registrations, memberships, payments, and payroll |
| Coordinators | Schedule activities, assign instructors, manage operations |
| Instructors | Conduct activities, report attendance, report maintenance issues |
| Maintenance Staff | Manage equipment repairs and maintenance activities |
| Customers | Register for activities, manage memberships, receive notifications |

The proposed system will integrate with existing organizational platforms where applicable.

# Summary

My Daniel is designed to become the central operational platform of Daniel Rowing Center. By integrating customer management, activity scheduling, attendance tracking, payroll validation, maintenance management, communication, and reporting into a single system, the organization will improve operational efficiency, reduce administrative overhead, and support future growth.

The system directly addresses the business problems identified during the organizational analysis phase and provides a scalable foundation for the center's long-term operational needs.
