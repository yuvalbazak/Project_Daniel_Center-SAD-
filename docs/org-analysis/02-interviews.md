# Interviews and Observations — Daniel Rowing Center

**Document:** Current State Analysis — Interviews and Observations  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Prepared by:** Project Team

---

# 1. Interview — Center Manager

**Interview Date:** March 23, 2026

## Role and Responsibilities

The Center Manager is responsible for the overall operation of Daniel Rowing Center, including professional activities, employee management, facility maintenance, equipment supervision, and financial management.

Daily responsibilities include:

- Monitoring ongoing activities
- Making managerial decisions
- Solving operational issues
- Supervising staff and coordinators
- Planning future activities and programs
- Monitoring revenues and expenses
- Improving operational efficiency

The role combines office-based work with active field supervision.

---

## Center Activities

The center serves two primary populations:

### Children and Youth

- Sailing
- Olympic Kayaking
- Academic Rowing

### Adult Members

- Academic Rowing
- Kayaking
- Sailing Activities
- Fitness Facilities

The center also operates Dragon Boat activities for companies, organizations, and rehabilitation groups.

---

## Information Systems in Use

The manager reported that the organization relies on several independent systems:

| System | Purpose |
|----------|----------|
| Fizikal | Membership and customer management |
| Priority | Accounting and financial management |
| Synel | Employee attendance reporting |
| Excel Files | Equipment and maintenance tracking |

Although these systems support daily operations, they are not fully integrated.

---

## Main Challenges

The manager identified several key challenges:

### Fragmented Information

Information is distributed across multiple systems and Excel files, making it difficult to obtain a complete and up-to-date view of organizational activities.

### Manual Processes

Many operational activities still require manual data synchronization and verification.

### Maintenance Management

Boat maintenance records are managed through Excel spreadsheets, limiting visibility and historical tracking capabilities.

### Training Efficiency

The manager identified a gap between the number of instructional hours invested and the actual operational outcomes.

---

## Improvement Opportunities

The manager highlighted the following improvement areas:

- Instructor workforce efficiency
- Resource management
- Information management
- Integration between systems
- Decision-making based on real-time data

---

# 2. Interview — Sailing Coordinator

**Interview Date:** April 6, 2026

## Role and Responsibilities

The Sailing Coordinator manages the day-to-day sailing activities and serves as the primary point of contact for instructors, participants, and parents.

Main responsibilities include:

- Activity scheduling
- Instructor coordination
- Participant management
- Parent communication
- Equipment supervision
- Operational issue resolution

---

## Registration Process

The current registration process is divided between two different employees:

### Coordinator Responsibilities

- Explaining activities
- Recommending suitable programs
- Providing pricing information
- Answering professional questions

### Accounting Manager Responsibilities

- Registration
- Payment collection
- Customer record management

This separation often creates delays, confusion, and communication gaps.

---

## Attendance Management

Attendance is currently managed manually through Google Sheets.

Instructors manually mark:

- Present participants
- Absent participants
- Repeated absences

The process depends entirely on manual updates.

---

## Instructor Scheduling

Most instructors are assigned to fixed groups and schedules.

When replacements are required:

1. The coordinator posts a request in a WhatsApp group.
2. Available instructors volunteer to replace the absent instructor.
3. If no replacement is found, the activity is postponed.

---

## Knowledge Management Issues

The coordinator reported significant dependency on personal knowledge.

Current issues include:

- No centralized database
- No shared knowledge repository
- No historical instructor records
- No documentation of participant progress
- No structured incident documentation

As a result, organizational knowledge is highly dependent on specific individuals.

---

## Parent Communication

Communication with parents is conducted mainly through:

- Phone calls
- Personal conversations
- WhatsApp groups

The coordinator acts as the primary communication channel.

This creates a risk that important information may not reach parents if communication is delayed.

---

## Equipment Maintenance Process

When equipment problems occur:

1. An instructor identifies the issue.
2. A photo is sent to the staff WhatsApp group.
3. The coordinator evaluates the issue.
4. Repairs are either performed internally or by an external technician.
5. Equipment availability is manually updated in Excel.

No centralized maintenance tracking system currently exists.

---

## Main Challenges

The coordinator identified the following issues:

- Fragmented registration process
- Manual attendance management
- Manual payroll validation
- Lack of centralized information
- Poor maintenance documentation
- Dependency on personal communication

---

# 3. Interview — Accounting Manager

**Interview Date:** April 20, 2026

## Role and Responsibilities

Although formally serving as the Accounting Manager, the role includes multiple operational responsibilities:

- Customer service
- Registration management
- Payment collection
- Payroll administration
- Supplier management
- Membership administration

---

## Customer Registration Process

The registration process begins with a phone conversation.

During the call:

1. The customer receives information about available activities.
2. Personal information is collected manually.
3. A trial lesson is scheduled.
4. Customer information is entered into Fizikal.
5. Payment is collected.
6. Following the trial lesson, the customer decides whether to continue.

The process relies heavily on manual data entry.

---

## Membership Management

The center operates through memberships and activity programs.

Memberships are generally sold for:

- Six months
- One year

Payments are usually divided into monthly installments.

The accounting department also manually monitors upcoming membership expirations and contacts customers regarding renewals.

---

## Discount Approval Process

Several customer groups are eligible for discounts:

- Soldiers
- Pensioners
- Single-parent families
- Large families

Customers submit supporting documents by email.

The Accounting Manager manually verifies eligibility and approves discounts.

---

## Problems with Discount Management

The current process presents several challenges:

- No automated validation
- No real-time verification
- High administrative workload
- Risk of unauthorized discounts
- Revenue loss due to incorrect approvals

---

## Payroll Management

Employees report working hours through Synel.

However, common issues include:

- Missing clock-ins
- Missing clock-outs
- Incorrect reporting
- Reporting errors

At the end of each month, attendance reports are manually compared with actual activity schedules.

The process is highly time-consuming and prone to human error.

---

## Data Analysis

Operational and financial reports are currently produced through manual analysis of exported data from multiple systems.

Most analytical work is performed using Excel spreadsheets.

---

## Main Challenges

The Accounting Manager identified the following challenges:

- Multiple disconnected systems
- Manual data processing
- Discount management inefficiencies
- Payroll reporting inaccuracies
- Limited business intelligence capabilities

---

# 4. Observations

## Customer Registration Process

The registration process begins with a phone inquiry from a potential customer.

Key observations:

- Customer information is collected manually.
- Trial lessons are required before full registration.
- Customer records are entered manually into Fizikal.
- Payment must be completed before participation.
- Additional registration steps are required after the trial lesson.

### Identified Issues

- Duplicate data entry
- Long registration process
- High dependency on staff availability
- Risk of data-entry errors

---

## Payroll Reporting Process

Employees report attendance using the Synel application.

Key observations:

- Employees frequently forget to report attendance.
- Clock-in and clock-out times are often inaccurate.
- Attendance corrections require manual intervention.
- Monthly payroll validation is performed manually.

### Identified Issues

- Time-consuming validation process
- Payroll inaccuracies
- High administrative workload
- Dependence on manual verification

---

## Equipment Maintenance Process

Equipment maintenance currently relies on WhatsApp communication and Excel tracking.

Key observations:

- Instructors report issues through WhatsApp.
- Equipment status is manually tracked.
- Maintenance history is not centrally stored.
- Equipment availability is not updated automatically.

### Identified Issues

- Lack of maintenance visibility
- Missing historical records
- Limited tracking capabilities
- Delayed operational decisions

---

# 5. Summary

The interviews and observations revealed that the majority of operational challenges stem from fragmented information systems, manual processes, and the absence of a centralized information repository.

The most significant pain points include:

- Customer registration management
- Payroll reporting and validation
- Equipment maintenance tracking
- Knowledge management
- Parent communication
- Data accessibility and reporting

These findings support the need for a centralized information system capable of integrating customers, employees, activities, payroll, and equipment management into a single platform.
